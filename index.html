Did You Know?

F1 brakes can reach temperatures of 1000°C – hot enough to glow red in the dark during night races.

Drivers lose an average of 4kg of body weight per race due to extreme physical exertion and heat.

Modern F1 engines rev up to 15,000 RPM and produce over 1000 horsepower from just 1.6 liters.

An F1 car can go from 0 to 100 km/h and back to 0 in less than 5 seconds combined.
